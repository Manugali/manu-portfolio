---
title: "On explaining yourself"
excerpt: "The version of you that interviews well is not always the version that feels true."
date: "2025-06-20"
tags: ["reflections", "life"]
---

There is a version of my story that fits neatly: master's degree, enterprise engineer, reliable, technical, professional.

It is not a lie. It is just incomplete.

In interviews and on LinkedIn, you learn to sound certain. You compress years into bullet points. You smile about challenges. You do not mention the weeks you felt lost, or the friendships that thinned out, or the nights you wondered if you picked the right country, city, career — all of it at once.

I am proud of what I have built. I also do not think pride and doubt cancel each other out.

I started writing here because I want at least one corner of the internet that does not need me to be impressive. Where I can write like someone who is still becoming whoever he is becoming.

If that resonates — if you are also trying to hold a career and a self at the same time — you are welcome here.
