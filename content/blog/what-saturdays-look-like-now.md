---
title: "What Saturdays look like now"
excerpt: "Small routines in West Des Moines. Nothing worth posting about, which is kind of the point."
date: "2025-03-28"
tags: ["life", "notes"]
---

I work Monday through Friday like most people. Saturdays are when I remember I am a person and not a calendar invite.

Usually it is slow. Coffee. Groceries. Maybe a long walk if Iowa weather is cooperating. Sometimes I cook something overly ambitious and eat half of it. Sometimes I do not leave the apartment and feel guilty about that, then stop feeling guilty.

I am not great at hobbies that photograph well. I do not have a hot take about productivity. I just have these days — uneven, ordinary — and I am trying to let them be enough.

Work can swallow the week if you let it. Especially when the problems are interesting and the people depend on you. But I am worse at everything, including the work itself, when I treat rest like something I have to earn first.

This is not advice. It is a note to myself, posted publicly for no particular reason.
